auto-cycling queries

Every word looks at every other word and decides how much to borrow from each. Click a word to make it the query, then watch which words it leans on. Notice the payoff: the pronoun it resolves to a different word when the last word of the sentence changes.

attention weights  from  it
QUERY (Q)
What it is looking for: a learned "question" vector that probes every other word.
KEYS (K)
Each word's "label." The query compared against every key gives a raw match score per word.
VALUES (V)
The content pulled back, blended by the weights above. A heavier blue line means more of that word flows in.
•The match scores are squashed through softmax so the weights for one query always sum to 100 percent. Weights here are illustrative, not from a live model, but they follow how real attention behaves: pronouns latch onto their referent, verbs onto their subject and object.
