2. Embeddings

Each token becomes a point in "meaning space", where similar ideas sit close together.

Word embeddings

Meaning is a place in space

Every word becomes a point. Words that mean similar things drift to the same neighborhood. Watch them sort themselves, then hover a word to see its closest neighbors.

Press Cluster to sort the words by meaning, then hover any word.
Hover a word for nearest neighbors
Why this matters. Distance in this space equals difference in meaning. To search by idea instead of by keyword, you turn the query into a point and grab whatever sits nearest. That nearest-neighbor lookup is exactly what powers semantic search and the retrieval step in RAG.
Note: real embeddings live in hundreds of dimensions. This is an honest 2D flattening, so the relative neighborhoods hold even though the exact distances are simplified.

How it writes the answer

4. Next-token prediction & sampling

The model scores every possible next token, then picks one. Temperature dials the randomness.

How a model picks the next word

A language model does not know the next word. For each blank it scores every candidate, turns those scores into probabilities, then draws one at random in proportion to those probabilities. Temperature reshapes the draw: low concentrates almost all the weight on the favorite, high flattens it so unlikely words get a real chance.

How it learned in the first place

5. Backpropagation

Training: the model guesses, measures its error, and sends that error backward to nudge every weight.

How a neural network learns

A guess flows forward (left to right), gets compared to the target to measure the error, then that error travels backward (right to left) and nudges every connection to do a little better. Repeat, and the error shrinks.

Illustrative animation: the activations and weight changes are simulated for intuition, not exact math. The directions and the shrinking error are faithful to how training works.
